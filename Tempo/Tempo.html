Curating a "Third Space experience" based on moods and emotions


As the technology of autonomous cars grows closer and closer to reality, it won't be far in experiencing a new user experience space- the time and space spent within the car. The "Third Space" project explores what the experience might look like- particularly between home and work.

CONTEXT

The client


For our junior spring semester, FCA approached our product design studio in order to develop a concept for the Third Space, an automobile experience between home and work. Through this semester-long project, my group worked through collecting user data with exploratory design methods to provide a futuristic concept.



For this project, I contributed to the ideation and was in charge of motion graphics and concept development for our solution, Tempo. With a near futures with autonomous vehicles, how can drivers curate a new experience from their every drives between home and work?

PROBLEM

How might we...


How might we design an educational game around kitchen regulation in order to make learning more engaging and increase student retention rates while also addressing lower reading levels?

Solution


CK Challenge, a tablet game application, fits into the existing class structure by giving the instructor the ability to set the pace of the day. The application is split into the exam’s ten chapters, with three educational sections for each chapter.

PROCESS

CK Challenge is the product of a fifteen week process moving through research, visioning, and four stages of prototyping and usability testing. We are thankful to have worked with a dedicated non-profit organization to bring their students closer to success.

RESEARCH

Interviews


In order to learn more about our client and our client’s needs, we interviewed key stakeholders and observed how classes were conducted. We were able to visit the site and interview some students and instructors.
